Students

Douglas C. McCall

Years in Program: 1st Year

Contact Email: dcm68@psu.edu

Age: 43

Hometown: Baldwinsville, NY

Education:
B. Mus. Music Education –SUNY Potsdam (NY)
M.Mus. Music Education –Ithaca College (NY)
CAS Educational Administration –SUNY Oswego (NY)

What were you doing prior to Penn State:
I was teaching full-time as a 9-12 Choral Music educator. I also was (still am) the Director of Music for the Baldwinsville First United Methodist Church

What factors influenced your decision to pursue this degree:
I have always known that I wanted to teach at the collegiate level which typically requires a terminal degree. After conducting some informal research in my own classroom, I fell in love with the research process which cemented my decision to pursue my Ph.D.

Why Penn State:
I have started my Ph.D. two other times and Penn State was the first program that felt like “home.” From my first conversation with Dr. Linda Thornton, to my on-campus visit, I knew that Penn State was where I was meant to finish my Ph.D.

What is unique about the Penn State graduate music education program:
The small number of people in the program, couple with the number of faculty makes for a lot of opportunities for one-on-one interaction. The requirement for a cognate that is outside of the school of music encourages you to think about other ideas and methods that could impact your work.

What do you do to relax and have fun outside of the graduate program:
I enjoy finding timeto just enjoy a meal with the other doctoral students. I make time to play golf. And believe it or not, commuting home on the weekend to be with my family has become a great source of enjoyment for me.

What advice do you have for prospective students regarding the application process:
The application process, including the on-campus visit, is designed not only to help Penn State decide if you are a good fit, but equally as important, to help you decide if WE ARE a good fit. I have done this a couple oftimes, and it is definitely about finding a good fit.

What tips do you have for being successful in the program:
 Work hard, plan efficiently, and remember that not everything has to be done immediately...if you forget that you will drive yourself crazy.

William Oliver

Years in Program: 1st Year

Contact Email: wlo9@psu.edu

Age: 31

Hometown: Hollywood, Florida

Education:
B.S. in Music Education -Florida A&M University
M.M. in Music Education -Boston University

What were you doing prior to Penn State:
Teaching 9-12 Instrumental Music (Band) in Atlanta, Georgia

What factors influenced your decision to pursue this degree:
The desire to help clarify paths towards music administration for minority faculty in Higher Education.

Why Penn State:
Penn State has a track record of diversity and inclusion. They have faculty minority faculty across the school and also within the music department (i.e. music education faculty and private studio teachers)

What is unique about the Penn State graduate music education program:
It is a melting pot of opportunity. Though Penn State only offers a PhD in Music Education and DMA in Piano performance. The currently is making strides towards adding unique opportunities for students.

What do you do to relax and have fun outside of the graduate program:
Attending the football games and school athletic events provide an awesome balance to the rigor of the graduate curriculum. Volunteering with local high school band programs is relaxation for me as well.

What advice do you have for prospective students regarding the application process:
Do your research. When entering a graduate program (outside of what "they can do for you"). How can you make an impact on the campus? Ask yourself what unique experiences do you have that will add flavor to your experience in a new environment.

What tips do you have for being successful in the program:
 Use the help around you. Penn State University has a host of resources at your fingertips. Ask for help. It makes everyone have a sense of purpose when they can help others in the program.

Biography:
Mr. William P. Oliver, a native of Hollywood, FL, received his Bachelor of Science Degree in Music Education from Florida Agricultural and Mechanical University (FAMU), where many of his musical beliefs and practices were fostered and nurtured by Dr. William P. Foster, Dr. Julian E. White, and Dr. Shelby R. Chipman. Mr. Oliver completed his Master Degree in Music Education in 2014 at Boston University. As former appointed Director of Bands at Benjamin E. Mays High School, his teaching responsibilities included the Symphonic Band, Jazz Orchestra, Marching Band, and a host of chamber ensembles. Prior to his appointment at Benjamin E. Mays High School he served four years as the Assistant Director of Bands at Stephenson High School in Stone Mountain, Georgia. His professional affiliations include, Georgia Music Educators Associations, Phi Mu Alpha Sinfonia Fraternity of America Inc., and Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity Inc. Now a first semester student at Pennsylvania State University, William is pursing his PhD in Music Education with a research emphasis of Higher Education Music Administration. Outside of his teaching responsibilities, he is an active band clinician in the Metro-Atlanta area and a former bass trombonist in the Cobb Wind Symphony under the direction of Alfred L. Watkins.

Biography:
Mrs. Michquelena Potlunas Ferguson is currently a second-year Ph.D. Candidate in Music Education at Penn State University. Since the fall of 2017, she has served as a Graduate Assistant of the Penn State Marching Blue Band under the direction of Mr. Gregory Drane during her graduate studies. Mrs. Ferguson also holds a Masters of Music degree in Music Education with a Research Emphasis from West Chester University, a Masters of Business Administration degree fromAlvernia University, and is an honors graduate with a Bachelor of Science degree in Music Education from Penn State University.

Mrs. Ferguson’s research interests include investigating the role of women in secondary and collegiate bands, athletic band musicianship, and the interconnections between media usage and music. She has presented both clinical and research presentations at state, regional, and national music education conferences during her time as a graduate student at Penn State.

Prior to her doctoral studies at Penn State, Mrs. Ferguson taught in the Pennsylvania Public Schools for ten years, primarily in high school instrumental music. Most recently, Mrs. Ferguson served as the Senior High School Band Director and Music Department K-12 Chairperson in the Exeter Township School District, located just outside of Reading, PA. At Exeter Township, Mrs. Ferguson was responsible for directing the 200+ member non-competitive marching band, the concert band, the auditioned wind ensemble, jazz bands, AP Music Theory, and general music courses in popular music and music technology. In her role as the Music Department Chairperson, Mrs. Ferguson was responsible for overseeing curriculum writing and development goals for her department as well as serving as the liaison between administration, teachers, parents, and the community. She also conducted the pit orchestra for numerous productions. Mrs. Ferguson previously served as the Elementary Instrumental Music Teacher in the Exeter Township School District for two years in which she taught beginning string and band lessons to students in grades 3 and 4 throughout the district.

Mrs. Ferguson is an avid French horn player who has continually played as a free-lance musician for community bands, orchestras,and theaters throughout her professional career.
