Christiana Usenza

Years in Program: 1st Year

Contact Email: cau5@psu.edu

Age: 36

Hometown: Washington, VT

What tips do you have for being successful in the program:
 Don't be afraid to ask questions of your professors and fellow graduate students.


Education:
BA in Anthropology, Music, Dance- Goucher College.
MA in Ethnomusicology - Tufts University.


What were you doing prior to Penn State:
Over the six years between completing my masters and beginning my PhD, I was a practicing musician, teacher and community organizer. I had 25 private lesson students in Boston, I was a founding member and mentor for School of Honk, community school that taught New Orleans second line music. I was leading an all-women's rock band called Paper Waves. I was producing concerts for my series called Afropop Night Live in Boston. I volunteered with Girls Rock Boston, I volunteered with Afropop Worldwide - writing album reviews and producing podcasts. I worked at a non-profit organizing free music programming for Latino elders at FriendshipWorks, and I directed an after school music program at a non-profit called Hyde Square Task Force, leading a free music program for Latino teens.

What factors influenced your decision to pursue this degree:
I was ready to do more research in my area of interest: music of the African and Afro Latin diaspora in music education and community music in the United States. The degree will hopefully allow me to teach college, or take on a leadership role in a non-profit, or a research institution.

Why Penn State:
I sought to work with Dr. Clements and Dr. Watts whose work overlaps with ethnomusicology, as ethno-educators.

What is unique about the Penn State graduate music education program:
It is a progressive program open to ideas and diversity


What do you do to relax and have fun outside of the graduate program:
I have my own band called Athena and the Night Owls for which I write songs. I go hiking, listen to records, cook, play with my dog, go dancing, take yoga.

What advice do you have for prospective students regarding the application process:
Film yourself teaching!
