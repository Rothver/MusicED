What advice do you have for prospective students regarding the application process:
Music education at the graduate level has nothing to do with music nor education. It is a total disconnect to even Penn State's undergraduate music education program. Everything you've prepared for your entire life is inconsequential. All those hours you spent in the practice room were for naught. Any experience you've had teaching has been a waste of time. All that matters is research and researchers incestuously lauding each others' research.


Gregg Mauroni is an adjunct professor at Penn State’s University Park campus, Mr. Mauroni is honored to be the new conductor of Essence of Joy, one of five choral ensembles in the Penn State School of Music. Mr. Mauroni’s current studies in Music Education explore the betterment of wellbeing through choral participation. His research seeks to illustrate how participation in a choral ensemble may be beneficial in allseven dimensions of wellness, including physical, mental, and emotional wellnesses. Further research areas include life-long music making through community ensembles and maximizing expressivity in choral performance. Mr. Mauroni is enjoying his 11thseason as conductor of the Harrisburg regional choir Voices of the Valley. He is the Assistant Director of Music and Arts at Monumental AME Church in Steelton, PA. As Music Director for musical theatre, he has conducted for Theatre Harrisburg, Open Stage of Harrisburg, and The Playhouse at Allenberry. He has enjoyed tenures at Roxbury High School in Succasunna, NJ and Central Dauphin East High School in Harrisburg, PA. He remains an invited accompanist, clinician, vocal coach, and commissioned arranger for many individual musicians and ensembles.
