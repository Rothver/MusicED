Throughout my time in the Ph.D program I learned that research can serve as a vehicle for both my academic and artistic aspirations. Originally from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Joey Harrell is a professional educator, performer, and researcher. He received a BM in Vocal Performance from Peabody Conservatory, a MM in Vocal Pedagogy from The Boston Conservatory, and is currently a proud Ph.D Candidate in Music Education for Musical Theatre Voice Pedagogy at Penn State University. At Penn State, his teaching duties include studio lessons for actors, group voice classes, and vocal pedagogy. As a practitioner and researcher, his research focuses on speech-based singing for voice building and habilitation as well as case study research with professional voice users seeking intervention for voice injury.

Joey has been a guest lecturer and clinician in Florida, Indiana and throughout the northeast. He has presented research on functional anatomy for breathing, cross-training the musical theatre performer, and understanding registration in musical theatre at several academic conferences. Outside of academia, Joey continues to perform in musical theatre and concert venues. He was most recently featured in the IPL Yuletide Celebration! with the Indianapolis Symphony, Children of Eden (Ensemble) at the John F. Kennedy Center, Oklahoma! At Quisisana Resort, and The Last Five Years (Jamie). In addition to teaching for MTCA, Joey maintains a private voice studio via Skype, allowing him to work with students all over the country. His students have been accepted into top BFA musical theatre programs and performed throughout the country in regional, national tour and Broadway productions.
