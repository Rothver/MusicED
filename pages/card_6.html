James Eldreth is pursuing a PhD in Music Education at The Pennsylvania State University. He previously taught elementary music in Missouri and holds certificates in Orff-Schulwerk, World Music Drumming, Little Kids Rock, and Montessori Arts. His scholarly interests include jazz pedagogy practices, children's musical play, the sociology of music, the uses of music to cope with poverty, and the impact of poverty on music education, and he has presented both nationally and internationally on jazz pedagogy in the elementary general music setting. He holds a Bachelor of Music Education, a Bachelor of Music in trumpet performance, and a Master of Arts in secondary education from Southeast Missouri State University, as well as a Master of Music Education from The Pennsylvania State University. In his free time, he enjoys playing jazz, spending time with his cats, and traveling with his wife.
